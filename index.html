Comparison of Accessibility to Electricity in 2011 & 2018

The following visualisation will compare the various accessibility to electricity for the major parts of the world. We can see that there is an increase in the number of accessibility as the world progresses.

GDP per person vs Invest Percentage among Countries

To know more the economy standing of the various countries, we explore the income per capita in international dollars. These data is adjusted for inflaction and differences in the cost of living between countries, so-called PPP dollars.
Also in the graph below, a variable for Investment percentage is added. This investment is describing the capital formation where it is used to describe the net capital accumulation during an accounting period for a particular country.
Generally, the higher the capital formation of an economy, the faster an economy can grow its aggregate income. With these two variables in hand, we would want to see the relationship that goes between 'GDP per person' and
'Invest percentage of the country' .As we can see, majority of the countries have a GDP per person but their investment rate is at a low +/- 20%. That being said, Bhutan is an outlier in this case with an extremely high investment percentage with a below
than average GDP per person.

How much has it changed since? This is where we will look at a visualisation that will depict the number of places in the world that gives the same rights to both men and women for several categories; Equal rights for employment,
remarry and living spaces. When we look at the numbers below, notice that there is an increasing number of countries from 2001 to 2019. Such figures would be deemed as a higher success rate in light of several feminist movements
