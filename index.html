GDP per person vs Invest Percentage among Countries

To know more the economy standing of the various countries, we explore the income per capita in international dollars. These data is adjusted for inflation and differences in the cost of living between countries, so-called PPP dollars.
Also in the graph below, a variable for Investment percentage is added. This investment is describing the capital formation where it is used to describe the net capital accumulation during an accounting period for a particular country.
Generally, the higher the capital formation of an economy, the faster an economy can grow its aggregate income. With these two variables in hand, we would want to see the relationship that goes between 'GDP per person' and
'Invest percentage of the country' .As we can see, majority of the countries have a high GDP per person but their investment rate is at a low +/- 20%. In comparison, Bhutan is an outlier in this case with an extremely high investment percentage with a below
than average GDP per person. That being said, much emphasis would need to go to the African countries where their income per capita is still among the lowests across all continents but yet, percentage of investments are on-par with other higher GDP per person countries.

Corruption

Corruption Index among Countries from 1996 - 2019

Corruption is a form of dishonesty or criminal offense undertaken by a person or an organization entrusted with a position of authority, to acquire illicit benefit or abuse power for one's private gain.
There have been many instances of corruption all over the world from 1MDB fund with Malaysia's former Prime Minister to Myanmar's dirty jade business. These acts of corruption apparently are in the minds of everyday citizens
as these issues could lead up to damaging the country economically and eventually decreases the public's trust on the governing body. With that, protest and instability could happen frequently in these countries.
With, we would take a look at 20 countries to see if they have made any improvements on dealing with corruption from 1996 till 2019. As can be seen in the heat map below, countries that acquire a low corruption index tend to remain low despite being more than 20 years apart.

Gender Equality

Equality among the sexes has always been a common topic especially in today's context. United Nation calls for it to be not only a fundamental right, but a necessary foundation for a peaceful, prosperous and sustainable world.
How much has it changed since? This is where we will look at a visualisation that will depict the number of places in the world that gives the same rights to both men and women for several categories; Equal rights to be the head of the household, employment,
travelling,remarry and living rights. When we look at the numbers below, notice that there is an increasing number of countries from 2001 to 2019. Such figures would be deemed as a success number in light of several feminist movements
happening all around the world. With that, we hope that in the few few years to come, more countries would join in the ranks to addressing equality among the sexes.
